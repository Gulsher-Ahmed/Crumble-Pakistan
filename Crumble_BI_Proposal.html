Section 01

Executive Summary

Crumble Pakistan has built one of the most exciting premium confectionery brands in the country — operating across four major cities, serving thousands of customers daily through branch operations, third-party delivery, and a growing e-commerce channel. With monthly limited-edition product launches and aggressive multi-platform marketing, Crumble has reached a scale where gut-feel decision-making is no longer sufficient.

Today, critical business data is fragmented across disconnected systems. Sales data lives in POS databases. Marketing spend data sits in individual ad platform dashboards. Branch performance, product launch impact, and supply chain metrics are tracked through manual processes and siloed spreadsheets. There is no single source of truth, no way to correlate a marketing rupee spent with a cookie sold, and no mechanism to understand — in real time — which decisions drive growth and which drain resources.

What I Will Deliver

  • A centralized data foundation that connects every revenue stream, every marketing channel, every branch, and every product into a single, clean, reliable data ecosystem
  • Live, interactive dashboards across Sales, Marketing, Operations, Supply Chain, Production, and Quality Assurance — accessible in real-time, from anywhere
  • Cross-department intelligence that correlates marketing spend with actual sales, campaign performance with product launches, and branch operations with customer behavior
  • An executive command center giving the Founder a single-screen view of the entire business — with automated alerts when any KPI crosses a threshold
  • Microsoft Dynamics 365 integration — architected from Day 1 to seamlessly absorb the upcoming Dynamics implementation
  • An AI-powered intelligence layer — leveraging artificial intelligence for demand forecasting, anomaly detection, natural language querying, and automated insight generation
"Within 6 months, Crumble will operate with the same data infrastructure that companies 10x its size invest millions to achieve — but purpose-built for the unique complexity of a multi-city, multi-channel, rapid-launch confectionery brand."

Understanding Your Business

Through detailed discovery sessions with Crumble's leadership and department heads — including conversations with Marketing, Performance Marketing, Creative, Branch Operations, Supply Chain, Production, and Quality Assurance teams — I have developed a comprehensive understanding of how Crumble operates and where intelligence gaps are hurting the business.

Multi-City Operations

Crumble currently operates physical branches across Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Lahore, and Faisalabad — each with distinct customer demographics, demand patterns, and operational characteristics.

Multi-Channel Revenue Model

ChannelApprox. ShareCharacteristics
Branch Sales (In-store + Delivery)~47%Highest margin, direct customer relationship
Blink (3rd Party Delivery)~31%Rapidly growing, commission-heavy, volume-driven
Website / E-commerce (crumble.pk + Apps)~22%Nationwide reach, digital funnel optimization opportunity

Aggressive Digital Marketing

Crumble runs active paid campaigns across Meta (Facebook + Instagram), Google (Search + Display), and TikTok (In-Feed + TopView) — with significant monthly spend that currently lacks unified ROI tracking and cross-channel attribution.

Monthly Product Launch Cycle

One of Crumble's most distinctive competitive advantages is its monthly limited-edition product launch program. This creates urgency and repeat traffic but also demands rapid performance tracking. Currently, there is no systematic framework to measure the true impact of each launch.

Upcoming Technology Investments

Crumble is planning to implement Microsoft Dynamics 365 as its core ERP. The BI infrastructure I build will be architected to integrate seamlessly with Dynamics once it goes live, ensuring zero disruption and immediate analytics capability over the new system.

Critical Intelligence Gaps

No Centralized Ad Spend & ROI Monitoring

Each marketing channel operates as an island. When asking "What is my actual return on last month's total marketing investment?" — there is no single place to find the answer.

No Cross-Department Correlation

When a new product launches, marketing spends, operations adjusts, production scales — but no one can see the full chain of cause and effect. Decisions are made in silos.

No Product Launch Impact Measurement

No standardized framework to measure: Did the launch drive incremental revenue or shift demand? Which channel was most effective? How did it perform across cities?

No Real-Time Visibility

Leadership relies on periodic manual reports that are often days old. Delayed data means delayed decisions.

POS Data is Locked

The POS system contains every transaction, every product sold, every time-of-day pattern — but this data is trapped with no analytics layer. Unlocking it is the single highest-value action in this engagement.

No AI or Predictive Capability

There is currently zero use of artificial intelligence or machine learning across the business. No demand forecasting, no anomaly detection, no automated insights. In 2026, this is a significant missed opportunity — especially for a brand with rich, pattern-heavy data ideal for AI-driven prediction.

The Cost of the Status Quo: Every month without centralized intelligence, Crumble is over-spending on underperforming channels, under-investing in high-ROAS channels, launching products without a feedback loop, missing AI-driven opportunities that competitors will exploit, and losing the compound advantage of accumulated business intelligence.

AI-Powered Intelligence Layer

Beyond traditional BI dashboards, this engagement introduces an artificial intelligence layer that transforms Crumble from a company that looks at data into one that learns from data. This is the differentiator that puts Crumble years ahead of any competitor in the Pakistani F&B space.

6.1 — AI-Powered Demand Forecasting

CapabilityDescription
Product Launch Demand PredictionUsing historical launch data, seasonal patterns, marketing spend, and city-level demand signals, the AI model predicts expected demand for each new monthly launch — enabling precise production planning and inventory allocation.
Revenue ForecastingMachine learning models that predict daily, weekly, and monthly revenue at the branch, city, and national level — with confidence intervals. Replaces guesswork with data-backed projections.
Seasonal & Event-Driven ForecastingThe model learns from Ramadan, Eid, weekends, weather patterns, and local events to predict demand spikes and dips — allowing proactive staffing, inventory, and marketing decisions.

6.2 — Anomaly Detection & Smart Alerts

CapabilityDescription
Sales Anomaly DetectionAI continuously monitors sales data across branches and flags unusual patterns: unexpected dips, sudden spikes, products selling out faster than predicted.
Marketing Spend AnomaliesAutomatically detects when campaign performance deviates significantly from historical norms — catching underperforming campaigns before they drain resources.
Operational AnomaliesFlags supply chain delays, production efficiency drops, or quality score declines that deviate from normal patterns.

6.3 — Automated Insight Generation

CapabilityDescription
Daily AI-Generated InsightsEvery morning, the system generates a natural-language summary of the most important things that happened yesterday — delivered directly to leadership.
Root Cause AnalysisWhen a KPI changes direction, the AI suggests why. "Islamabad branch revenue dropped 12% this week. Likely driver: Meta ad spend was paused on Tuesday."
Recommendation EngineData-backed recommendations: "Based on the last 4 launches, TikTok outperforms Meta for first-week awareness by 23%. Consider increasing TikTok allocation."

6.4 — Natural Language Querying

CapabilityDescription
Ask Questions in Plain EnglishUsing Power BI's AI-powered Q&A capability, any authorized user can type or speak questions like "What was our ROAS on Meta last week?" and get instant visual answers.
Conversational AnalyticsLeadership can interact with data conversationally, making BI accessible to non-technical team members and dramatically increasing adoption.

6.5 — Future AI Expansion Opportunities

  • Customer Segmentation AI — Automatically segment customers by behavior, purchase patterns, and lifetime value
  • Pricing Optimization — AI-driven pricing recommendations based on demand elasticity and competition
  • Marketing Mix Modeling — Advanced AI model for optimal budget allocation across channels
  • Churn Prediction — Identify customers at risk of not reordering and trigger retention campaigns
  • Social Sentiment Analysis — AI monitoring of social media mentions and reviews
AI is not a future ambition. It is built into the architecture from Day 1. The data foundation laid in Phase 1 is specifically designed to feed AI models. By Month 6, Crumble will have AI capabilities that most Pakistani companies won't achieve for years.
