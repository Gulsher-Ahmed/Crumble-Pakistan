Dear Agha Usman,

Following our recent discussion, I've tightened the Phase 1 scope so we stay focused strictly on Sales and Marketing, while ensuring the output remains commercially useful and not just another reporting layer.

At this stage, the goal is not to widen coverage, but to build a reliable decision layer around revenue, product movement, and marketing effectiveness.

01

Approach

The first step will be to understand what is actually driving movement in the business, and then structure reporting around those drivers.

The system will aim to answer:

What is driving revenue movement?

Which products or campaigns are genuinely adding value?

What is real performance versus growth that appears inflated due to an expanding base?

02

Sales Side

What is happening in revenue — and why?

The sales layer will be structured to reflect how the business actually operates.

Core Revenue Tracking

  • Hourly, daily, weekly, and monthly revenue trends
  • Daypart contribution
  • AOV and ticket size distribution
  • Branch-level and product-level movement
  • Trend shifts in demand, not just top sellers

LTO / Monthly Drop Intelligence

This will be treated as a separate analytical layer rather than being merged into general product reporting. The system will track:

  • Revenue contribution of each monthly LTO
  • Units sold and share within category
  • Performance comparison against previous LTO launches
  • Repeat potential of previously launched cookies
  • Whether an LTO is generating incremental sales or shifting demand from existing products
  • Performance curve post-launch (spike → stabilization → decline)

Product Behaviour

  • Product lifecycle behaviour
  • Cannibalization between items
  • Price-tier behaviour across classic, special, and LTO
  • Attachment patterns (e.g., whether certain cookies increase soft serve or shake sales alongside them)
03

External Drivers

What influences demand beyond the product itself?

This layer will help explain variations that standard reporting does not capture.

Weather / Temperature Impact

For a product like cookies, and especially for soft serve and shakes, external conditions can materially influence demand. This analysis will include:

  • Sales against temperature bands
  • Weather sensitivity by category
  • Impact of colder days on shakes and soft serve contribution
  • Product-level resilience across different conditions
  • Whether campaign timing should be adjusted based on expected weather patterns

Calendar Behaviour

  • Weekend vs weekday patterns
  • Start-of-month, mid-month, and end-of-month spending shifts
  • Impact of monthly LTO release timing
  • Seasonal and holiday effects
04

Marketing Side

Given the follower base is continuously growing, raw metrics alone will not provide accurate insights.

Normalization

  • Engagement relative to follower base
  • Reach relative to audience size
  • Saves and shares relative to exposure
  • Baseline-adjusted post performance

Content Breakdown

  • Reels vs carousels vs static vs stories
  • Hook strength and retention behaviour
  • Content that drives attention vs content that drives action
  • Separation of creative quality vs distribution-driven performance

Trend Direction

  • Month-on-month trajectory
  • Performance acceleration or slowdown
  • Signs of content fatigue
  • Whether growth is broad-based or driven by isolated spikes
05

Connecting Marketing to Sales

Initial Phase 1 linkage will focus on:

  • Comparing launch periods versus baseline periods
  • Understanding lag between content push and sales response
  • Evaluating whether specific formats or campaigns support LTO performance
  • Identifying whether demand lift is sustained or short-term
  • Mapping campaign timing against revenue movement where relation exists

Note

This layer will begin as exploratory. Any factors identified as having direct or indirect relationships will be incorporated into the reporting structure.
