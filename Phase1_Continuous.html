Dear Agha,

Following our recent discussions, I have refined the Phase 1 scope so that it remains focused specifically on Sales and Marketing, while ensuring the output is practical, commercially useful, and decision-oriented rather than just another reporting layer.

At this stage, the priority is not to expand coverage, but to build a reliable decision-support layer around revenue, product movement, and marketing effectiveness.

01

Approach

Phase 1 will not be approached as a dashboard-only exercise. The first priority will be to understand what is actually driving movement in the business and then structure reporting around those business drivers.

The aim is to answer questions such as:

What is driving revenue movement?

Which products or campaigns are genuinely creating value?

What reflects real performance, versus growth that appears stronger simply because the overall base has increased?

02

Sales Analytics

The sales layer will be designed to reflect how the business actually operates.

Core Revenue Tracking

  • Hourly, daily, weekly, and monthly revenue trends
  • Daypart contribution
  • AOV and ticket size distribution
  • Branch-level and product-level performance
  • Demand shifts and emerging trends, not just top sellers

LTO / Monthly Drop Intelligence

This will be treated as a dedicated analytical layer rather than being merged into general product reporting. The system will track:

  • Revenue contribution of each monthly LTO
  • Units sold and category share
  • Performance comparison against previous LTO launches
  • Repeat potential of previously launched cookies
  • Whether an LTO is generating incremental sales or shifting demand away from existing products
  • Performance curve after launch, including spike, stabilization, and decline

Product Behaviour

  • Product lifecycle tracking
  • Cannibalization between items
  • Price-tier analysis across Classic, Special, and LTO
  • Attachment patterns, including whether certain cookies increase soft serve or shake sales alongside them
03

External Drivers Analysis

This layer will help explain demand variation beyond internal business factors.

Weather / Temperature Analysis

  • Sales performance across temperature bands
  • Category-level sensitivity across cookies, soft serve, and shakes
  • Product-level resilience under different conditions
  • Impact of weather on category contribution
  • Whether campaign timing should be adjusted based on expected weather effect

Calendar Analysis

  • Weekday versus weekend performance
  • Start-of-month, mid-month, and end-of-month spending patterns
  • Impact of LTO release timing
  • Seasonal and holiday effects
04

Marketing Analytics

Normalized Performance Metrics

  • Engagement relative to follower base
  • Reach relative to audience size
  • Saves and shares relative to exposure
  • Baseline-adjusted post performance

Content Performance

  • Reels versus carousels versus static posts versus stories
  • Hook strength and retention patterns
  • Content that drives attention versus content that drives action
  • Separation of creative quality from distribution effect

Trend Analysis

  • Month-on-month trajectory
  • Growth acceleration or slowdown
  • Content fatigue indicators
  • Whether growth is broad-based or dependent on isolated spikes
05

Marketing to Sales Linkage

The initial Phase 1 analysis will also include:

  • Mapping campaign timing against revenue movement
  • Comparing launch periods against baseline periods
  • Understanding the lag between content push and sales response
  • Evaluating whether specific formats or campaigns are supporting LTO performance
  • Identifying whether demand lift is sustained or short term

Note

This part will begin as an exploratory layer. Where meaningful relationships are identified during Phase 1, they will be incorporated into the reporting structure.

Phase 1 will be handled independently on my end within the agreed BI scope. This includes:

At the same time, timely source access, business context, stakeholder validation, and support during clarification cycles would be required from the relevant internal teams to keep execution moving efficiently.

07

Data Layer & Pipeline Ownership

For Phase 1, the expectation is that I will build the reporting pipeline required for the agreed dashboards, including extraction logic from current source systems, cleaning and transformation rules, exception handling, and documentation of how data flows into the reporting layer.

Until D365 is live and stable, the existing systems will be used as interim source points. Once D365 is operational, it can become the primary reporting source where relevant and feasible within the Phase 1 timeline.

Where data quality issues or inconsistencies are identified, I will diagnose them, document them, raise them clearly, and support resolution from the reporting side. Source-system data generation, business-side data entry, and correction at the point of origin would remain with the relevant internal owners.

Objective

The objective in Phase 1 is to reduce manual follow-up dependency by establishing clear input expectations, data flow logic, and SOPs. Full cross-department operational enforcement or ERP implementation is not included in this engagement.
