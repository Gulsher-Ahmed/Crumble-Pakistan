## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Crumble Pakistan has built one of the most exciting premium confectionery brands in the country — operating across four major cities, serving thousands of customers daily through branch operations, third-party delivery (Blink), and a growing e-commerce channel. With monthly limited-edition product launches, aggressive marketing across Meta, Google, and TikTok, and a rapidly expanding operational footprint, Crumble has reached a scale where **gut-feel decision-making is no longer sufficient**.

Today, critical business data is fragmented across disconnected systems. Sales data lives in POS databases. Marketing spend data sits in individual ad platform dashboards. Branch performance, product launch impact, and supply chain metrics are tracked through manual processes and siloed spreadsheets. **There is no single source of truth**, no way to correlate a marketing rupee spent with a cookie sold, and no mechanism to understand — in real time — which decisions are driving growth and which are draining resources.

**This proposal outlines a 6-month engagement to build a comprehensive, enterprise-grade Business Intelligence infrastructure for Crumble Pakistan** — one that will transform how the leadership team makes decisions, how departments collaborate on shared data, and how the company scales.

### What I Will Deliver

- **A centralized data foundation** that connects every revenue stream, every marketing channel, every branch, and every product into a single, clean, reliable data ecosystem
- **Live, interactive dashboards** across Sales, Marketing, Operations, Supply Chain, Production, and Quality Assurance — accessible in real-time, from anywhere
- **Cross-department intelligence** that correlates marketing spend with actual sales, campaign performance with product launches, and branch operations with customer behavior
- **An executive command center** that gives the Founder a single-screen view of the entire business — with automated alerts when any KPI crosses a threshold
- **Microsoft Dynamics 365 integration** — architected from Day 1 to seamlessly absorb the upcoming Dynamics implementation into the BI ecosystem
- **An AI-powered intelligence layer** — leveraging artificial intelligence for demand forecasting, anomaly detection, natural language querying, and automated insight generation

### The Outcome

Within 6 months, Crumble will operate with the same data infrastructure that companies 10x its size invest millions to achieve — but purpose-built for the unique complexity of a multi-city, multi-channel, rapid-launch confectionery brand.

> *"The goal is not dashboards. The goal is decisions made faster, with more confidence, resulting in more revenue and less waste."*

## 2. UNDERSTANDING YOUR BUSINESS

Through detailed discovery sessions with Crumble's leadership and department heads — including conversations with the Marketing, Performance Marketing, Creative, Branch Operations, Supply Chain, Production, and Quality Assurance teams — I have developed a comprehensive understanding of how Crumble operates and where intelligence gaps are costing the business.

### The Crumble Ecosystem

**Multi-City Operations**
Crumble currently operates physical branches across **Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Lahore, and Faisalabad** — each with distinct customer demographics, demand patterns, and operational characteristics. Understanding per-branch performance and city-level trends is essential for expansion planning and resource allocation.

**Multi-Channel Revenue Model**
Revenue flows through three primary channels:

| Channel | Approx. Share | Characteristics |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Branch Sales** (In-store + Delivery) | ~47% | Highest margin, direct customer relationship |
| **Blink** (3rd Party Delivery) | ~31% | Rapidly growing, commission-heavy, volume-driven |
| **Website / E-commerce** (crumble.pk + Apps) | ~22% | Nationwide reach, digital funnel optimization opportunity |

**Aggressive Digital Marketing**
Crumble runs active paid campaigns across **Meta (Facebook + Instagram)**, **Google (Search + Display)**, and **TikTok (In-Feed + TopView)** — with significant monthly spend that currently lacks unified ROI tracking and cross-channel attribution.

**Monthly Product Launch Cycle**
One of Crumble's most distinctive competitive advantages is its **monthly limited-edition product launch program** — a new flavor or product released each month for a defined period. This creates urgency and repeat traffic but also demands rapid performance tracking to understand what's resonating and what's not. Currently, there is no systematic framework to measure the true impact of each launch on revenue, customer acquisition, and channel performance.

**Upcoming Technology Investments**
Crumble is planning to implement **Microsoft Dynamics 365** as its core ERP — a significant investment that will centralize finance, inventory, and operations data. The BI infrastructure I build will be architected to integrate seamlessly with Dynamics once it goes live, ensuring zero disruption and immediate analytics capability over the new system.

## 3. CURRENT STATE ASSESSMENT

Based on my discovery sessions and data environment review, the following gaps represent the most significant barriers to data-driven decision-making at Crumble:

### 3.1 — Fragmented Data Landscape

| Data Source | Current State | Impact |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| POS System (Ackman/Wizzman) | Data exists in raw database; no analytics layer | Revenue, transaction, and product data is inaccessible for analysis without manual extraction |
| Shopify / Website | Separate platform with its own reporting | E-commerce performance disconnected from branch sales view |
| Blink (3rd Party Delivery) | Platform-native reports only | Cannot correlate delivery channel performance with overall revenue mix |
| Meta Business Suite | Platform-level metrics | Spend and ROAS visible only within Meta, not connected to actual sales |
| Google Ads Manager | Platform-level metrics | Same isolation — no correlation with downstream conversions |
| TikTok Ads Manager | Platform-level metrics | Newest channel with least visibility into true ROI |
| Spreadsheets & Manual Reports | Various departments | Labour-intensive, error-prone, delayed, and inconsistent |

### 3.2 — Critical Intelligence Gaps

**No Centralized Ad Spend & ROI Monitoring**
Each marketing channel operates as an island. When Agha Usman asks *"What is my actual return on last month's total marketing investment across all channels?"* — there is no single place to find that answer. It requires pulling data from 3+ platforms, manually combining it, and hoping the attribution logic is consistent.

**No Cross-Department Correlation**
When a new product launches, marketing spends heavily to promote it. Branch operations sees increased footfall. Supply chain adjusts inventory. Production scales up. But **no one can see the full chain of cause and effect** in a single view. Decisions are made in silos, leading to overproduction, understocking, misallocated budgets, and missed opportunities.

**No Product Launch Impact Measurement**
Crumble's monthly limited-edition launches are a core part of the brand strategy — yet there is no standardized framework to measure:
- Did the launch drive incremental revenue or just shift demand from existing products?
- Which marketing channel was most effective for this specific launch?
- How did the launch perform across different cities?
- Should this product become permanent, be repeated seasonally, or be retired?

**No Real-Time Visibility**
Leadership relies on periodic manual reports that are often days old by the time they're reviewed. In a fast-moving business where weekend promotions, social media trends, and citywide events can shift demand overnight, **delayed data means delayed decisions**.

**POS Data is Locked**
The Ackman/Wizzman POS system contains the most critical data asset — every transaction, every product sold, every branch, every time-of-day pattern. But this data is trapped in a database with no analytics, visualization, or accessible reporting layer. Unlocking it is the single highest-value action in this engagement.

**No AI or Predictive Capability**
There is currently zero use of artificial intelligence or machine learning across the business. No demand forecasting, no anomaly detection, no automated insights, no natural language data querying. In 2026, this is a significant missed opportunity — especially for a brand with recurring monthly launches that generate rich, pattern-heavy data ideal for AI-driven prediction.

### 3.3 — The Cost of the Status Quo

Every month without centralized intelligence, Crumble is:
- **Over-spending on underperforming channels** without knowing it
- **Under-investing in high-ROAS channels** that could drive disproportionate returns
- **Launching products without a feedback loop** — unable to learn systematically from each release
- **Making branch-level and city-level decisions** on instinct rather than data
- **Missing AI-driven opportunities** that competitors will eventually exploit
- **Losing the compound advantage** that comes from 6, 12, 18 months of accumulated business intelligence

The BI infrastructure in this proposal does not just organize data. It **creates a competitive moat** — one that deepens every month as more data flows in, more patterns emerge, AI models improve, and better decisions compound.

## 6. AI-POWERED INTELLIGENCE LAYER

Beyond traditional BI dashboards, this engagement introduces an **artificial intelligence layer** that transforms Crumble from a company that *looks at data* into one that *learns from data*. This is the differentiator that puts Crumble years ahead of any competitor in the Pakistani F&B space.

### 6.1 — AI-Powered Demand Forecasting

| Capability | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Product Launch Demand Prediction** | Using historical launch data, seasonal patterns, marketing spend, and city-level demand signals, the AI model predicts expected demand for each new monthly launch — enabling precise production planning and inventory allocation before a product even goes live. |
| **Revenue Forecasting** | Machine learning models that predict daily, weekly, and monthly revenue at the branch, city, and national level — with confidence intervals. This replaces guesswork with data-backed projections for financial planning. |
| **Seasonal & Event-Driven Forecasting** | The model learns from Ramadan, Eid, weekends, weather patterns, and local events to predict demand spikes and dips — allowing proactive staffing, inventory, and marketing decisions. |

### 6.2 — Anomaly Detection & Smart Alerts

| Capability | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Sales Anomaly Detection** | AI continuously monitors sales data across branches and flags unusual patterns: unexpected dips, sudden spikes, products selling out faster than predicted, or revenue that deviates significantly from forecast. |
| **Marketing Spend Anomalies** | Automatically detects when a campaign's performance deviates significantly from historical norms — catching underperforming campaigns (or budget leaks) before they drain resources. |
| **Operational Anomalies** | Flags supply chain delays, production efficiency drops, or quality score declines that deviate from normal patterns — enabling fast intervention before problems compound. |

### 6.3 — Automated Insight Generation

| Capability | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Daily AI-Generated Insights** | Every morning, the system generates a natural-language summary of the most important things that happened yesterday — delivered directly to leadership. |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | When a KPI changes direction, the AI doesn't just flag it — it suggests why. *"Islamabad branch revenue dropped 12% this week. Likely driver: Meta ad spend in Islamabad was paused on Tuesday."* |
| **Recommendation Engine** | Data-backed recommendations generated automatically: *"Based on the last 4 product launches, TikTok outperforms Meta for first-week awareness by 23%. Consider increasing TikTok allocation for next launch."* |

### 6.4 — Natural Language Querying

| Capability | Description |
|------------|-------------|

| **Conversational Analytics** | Leadership can interact with data conversationally, making BI accessible to non-technical team members and dramatically increasing dashboard adoption across the organization. |

### 6.5 — Future AI Expansion Opportunities

Once the foundation is mature, the following AI capabilities can be layered on during the ongoing partnership phase:
